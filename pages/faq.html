Dysautonomia & POTS

What is dysautonomia? ▼

Dysautonomia is a disorder of the autonomic nervous system, which controls involuntary body functions like heart rate, blood pressure, digestion, and temperature regulation. It can cause a wide range of symptoms including dizziness, fatigue, heart palpitations, and digestive issues.

What is POTS? ▼

POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome) is a form of dysautonomia characterized by an abnormal increase in heart rate when standing up. Symptoms include dizziness, lightheadedness, fatigue, brain fog, and rapid heartbeat upon standing.

How is dysautonomia diagnosed? ▼

Diagnosis typically involves:

  • Tilt table test
  • Active standing test
  • Heart rate variability testing
  • Blood pressure monitoring
  • Comprehensive medical history and symptom assessment

Long Covid

What is Long Covid? ▼

Long Covid, also known as Post-Acute Sequelae of SARS-CoV-2 infection (PASC), refers to symptoms that persist for weeks or months after the initial COVID-19 infection. It can affect multiple organ systems and often includes dysautonomia-like symptoms.

How does Long Covid relate to dysautonomia? ▼

Many Long Covid patients develop dysautonomia symptoms, including POTS. The virus may damage the autonomic nervous system, leading to similar symptoms and management strategies as other forms of dysautonomia.
